## üìö HOMEWORK HELPER (When Homework Feels Hard)

### Step-by-Step Homework Plan
1. **Find a quiet spot** - Ask Mom for a place with no TV
2. **Get supplies** - Pencil, paper, books, whatever you need
3. **Set a timer** - Start with 15 minutes
4. **Work until timer beeps** - Try your best
5. **Take a 5-minute break** - Stand up, stretch, get water
6. **Do another 15 minutes** - If you have more work
7. **Done!** - Put homework in backpack

### When Homework Feels Impossible
**Try these tricks:**
- **Read the directions twice** - Sometimes the second time makes sense
- **Do the easy parts first** - Skip the hard ones for now
- **Ask Mom for help** - She might know (or can look it up)
- **Call or text Dad** - If Mom says it's okay, Dad can help over the phone
- **Write a note to the teacher** - "I tried but didn't understand" is honest
- **Do your best** - That's all Dad asks

### If You Forget Homework at School
It's okay. It happens. Tell your teacher in the morning. Don't lie about it.

## üò∞ WHEN YOU FEEL SAD, MAD, OR SCARED

### Big Feelings Are Normal
At Mom's house, you might feel:
- **Sad** - You miss Dad, you miss your stuff, you miss the weekend
- **Mad** - You don't want to be at two houses
- **Bored** - You don't have your LEGOs or your room at Dad's
- **Lonely** - You wish someone would play with you
- **Worried** - About school, friends, Mom and Dad, lots of things

**ALL OF THESE ARE OKAY. You're not bad for feeling this way.**

### What You Can Do Right Now (By Yourself)

#### 1. Breathing Exercise
- Breathe in slowly (count to 4)
- Hold it (count to 4)
- Breathe out slowly (count to 4)
- Do this 3 times
- You might feel a little calmer

#### 2. Tell Your Stuffed Animal
- Find any stuffed animal or toy
- Tell it how you feel
- It's okay if you cry
- The toy won't judge you

#### 3. Draw Your Feelings
- Get paper and crayons/pencil
- Draw how you feel (doesn't have to look like anything)
- Mad might be scribbles, sad might be rain
- You can rip it up after if you want

#### 4. Count Things
- Count backward from 100 (or as high as you can)
- Count all the things you can see that are blue
- Count your breaths
- This gives your brain something else to do

#### 5. Move Your Body
- Do 10 jumping jacks
- Run in place for 30 seconds
- Dance to a song in your head
- Shake your arms and legs
- Movement helps big feelings

### What to Say to Mom
If you need to talk to Mom about feelings:
- "I feel sad"
- "I miss Dad"
- "I need a hug"
- "Can I call Dad?"
- "I don't want to talk about it, but I'm upset"

**Mom loves you too. She wants to help even if she doesn't know how.**

## üéí SCHOOL SURVIVAL GUIDE

### If School Is Hard
1. **Ask your teacher for help** - That's their job
2. **Sit in the front** - It's easier to pay attention
3. **Be honest** - "I don't understand" is a good thing to say
4. **Take breaks** - Ask for bathroom break if you need a minute
5. **Do your best** - Nobody's perfect

### If Someone Is Mean to You
1. **Walk away** - You don't have to listen to mean people
2. **Tell a teacher** - They need to know
3. **Tell Mom** - She can help
4. **Tell Dad on Friday** - Dad will help figure it out
5. **Remember**: Their meanness is about them, not you

### If You Get in Trouble
1. **Tell the truth** - Don't lie to make it better
2. **Accept the consequence** - Say "okay" and move on
3. **Tell Mom** - Even if you're scared to
4. **Tell Dad** - Dad won't be mad at you for being honest
5. **Learn from it** - Try not to do it again

**You're going to make mistakes. That's how kids learn.**

### Make a Paper Chain
1. Get 4 strips of paper (Mom can help cut them)
2. Write one day on each: Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
3. Connect them in a chain (tape or staple)
4. Each morning, tear off one link
5. When the chain is gone, it's Friday!

### Or Draw It
Draw 4 boxes in a row. Each night before bed, color one in. When all 4 are colored, you see Dad tomorrow!
