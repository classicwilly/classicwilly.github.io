# MORNING TRANSITION PROTOCOL (MTP-SOP)

**Purpose**: Bridge the gap between sleep and active execution. This protocol transforms you from "just woke up" to "ready to execute" through structured physical, mental, and environmental transitions.

**Timing**: 6:00 AM - 7:00 AM daily (both Solo and Family modes)

**Duration**: 60 minutes (can compress to 45 minutes if needed)

**Integration**: Builds on Foundational Daily Protocols (water, pills) and leads into mode-specific execution (Solo or Family)

---

## PROTOCOL PHILOSOPHY

**Why this matters**: The neurodivergent brain needs structured transitions. Jumping from sleep to complex tasks causes:
- Decision paralysis (too many options)
- Executive dysfunction (can't initiate)
- Sensory overwhelm (environment not controlled)
- Cognitive fog (brain still waking up)

**This protocol removes those barriers through fixed sequence and automated decisions.**

---

## TRANSITION OVERVIEW

The morning is divided into 4 phases:
1. **Physical Activation** (6:00-6:15 AM) - Wake the body
2. **Environment Setup** (6:15-6:30 AM) - Create the workspace
3. **Mental Calibration** (6:30-6:45 AM) - Prime the brain
4. **Execution Launch** (6:45-7:00 AM) - Initiate first task

**Do NOT skip phases or reorder them.** Each phase prepares for the next.

---

## PHASE 1: PHYSICAL ACTIVATION (6:00-6:15 AM)

**Purpose**: Wake the body and stabilize physical systems.

### 6:00-6:02 AM - Wake & Water Protocol (Foundational)
- **Action**: 
  1. Alarm sounds at 6:00 AM
  2. Stand up immediately (do not hit snooze, do not check phone)
  3. Drink 16 oz (1 full glass) of water
- **Tool**: Water bottle filled night before, positioned at bedside
- **Why**: Dehydration mimics ADHD symptoms. Fix it immediately.
- **Non-Negotiable**: This must happen before any other action

### 6:02-6:03 AM - Pill Protocol (Foundational)
- **Action**:
  1. Take all pills from today's slot in pill organizer
  2. Visual check: Is tomorrow's slot filled?
- **Tool**: 7-day pill organizer at bedside or Command Center
- **Why**: Medication consistency is non-negotiable for executive function
- **Non-Negotiable**: Do this before looking at phone or screens

### 6:03-6:10 AM - Movement Activation
- **Action**: Execute ONE of these movement protocols:
  1. **Walk outside** (5-7 minutes around the block)
  2. **Stretching sequence** (7 minutes: neck, shoulders, back, legs)
  3. **Light exercise** (7 minutes: jumping jacks, push-ups, bodyweight squats)
  4. **Shower** (5-7 minutes, focus on physical sensation)
- **Pick the one that feels easiest today.** Movement quality doesn't matter. Just move.
- **Why**: Movement forces blood flow to the brain, activates alertness, breaks sleep inertia
- **Tool**: Pre-selected exercise routine or outdoor walking route
- **Non-Negotiable**: No screens until movement is complete

### 6:10-6:15 AM - Caffeine Protocol
- **Action**:
  1. Make coffee or tea (method of choice)
  2. Drink it slowly while standing or sitting at kitchen/Command Center
  3. Do NOT multitask (no phone, no reading, just drink)
- **Why**: Caffeine takes 15-30 minutes to activate. Start early. Mindful drinking prevents gulping and stomach issues.
- **Tool**: Coffee maker, kettle, or preferred caffeine source
- **Optional**: Add protein (protein shake, eggs, yogurt) if breakfast is part of routine

**Phase 1 Success Metric**: You are physically awake. Heart rate elevated. Water and medication in system. Caffeine initiated.

---

## PHASE 2: ENVIRONMENT SETUP (6:15-6:30 AM)

**Purpose**: Create a controlled, low-chaos workspace optimized for focus.

### 6:15-6:20 AM - Command Center Activation
- **Action**:
  1. Go to your designated Command Center (desk, home office, kitchen table)
  2. Clear any clutter from yesterday (papers, dishes, random objects)
  3. Arrange essential tools within reach:
     - Water bottle (refilled)
     - Headphones (charged)
     - Pill organizer (visible for tomorrow check)
     - Notebook/capture tool
     - Phone (silenced)
  4. Adjust lighting (dim overhead, use desk lamp if preferred)
  5. Adjust temperature (add/remove clothing layer as needed)
- **Why**: Physical environment affects cognitive load. Controlled space = reduced overwhelm.
- **Tool**: Command Center (your primary workspace)

### 6:20-6:25 AM - Digital Environment Setup
- **Action**:
  1. Open computer/laptop
  2. Close ALL tabs and applications from yesterday
  3. Open ONLY these tools:
     - Calendar (today's schedule)
     - Google Tasks (today's task list)
     - Primary work application (email, code editor, document processor)
  4. Set phone to Do Not Disturb (with emergency contacts allowed)
  5. Turn off notifications on computer (or use Focus mode)
- **Why**: Digital clutter = cognitive clutter. Start with clean slate.
- **Non-Negotiable**: Do NOT check social media, news, or non-essential apps yet

### 6:25-6:30 AM - Bubble Shield Deployment
- **Action**:
  1. Put on Soundcore Space One headphones
  2. **If Solo Mode starting today**: Profile 1.0 (Transparency 3, Vol 20%)
  3. **If Family Mode active**: Profile 2.0 (Transparency 5, Vol 10%)
  4. Test audio (play music, white noise, or silence - your choice)
  5. Adjust fit for comfort (loose enough to wear for hours)
- **Why**: Auditory protection prevents sensory overload and creates "bubble" for focus
- **Tool**: Soundcore Space One headphones (charged overnight)
- **Non-Negotiable**: Headphones stay on for rest of morning protocol

**Phase 2 Success Metric**: Command Center is clear, digital workspace is clean, Bubble Shield is deployed. You are in a controlled environment.

---

## PHASE 3: MENTAL CALIBRATION (6:30-6:45 AM)

**Purpose**: Orient your brain to today's priorities and prime executive function.

### 6:30-6:35 AM - Calendar Review
- **Action**:
  1. Open calendar and review today's schedule
  2. For each event, ask:
     - What time does it start/end?
     - What preparation does it require?
     - What is the transition cost? (time to get there, mental shift needed)
  3. Identify **time blocks available for deep work** (gaps between meetings)
  4. Identify **high-risk times** (back-to-back meetings, sensory-heavy events)
  5. Add missing essentials if needed:
     - Meal times
     - Buffer time before/after meetings
     - Breaks (5-10 min every 90 min)
- **Why**: Knowing the shape of your day prevents reactive chaos
- **Tool**: Calendar application
- **Output**: Mental model of "when I have focus time" and "when I have obligations"

### 6:35-6:42 AM - Task Triage & Priority Selection
- **Action**:
  1. Open Google Tasks and review your task list
  2. Categorize tasks by urgency:
     - **Today (Must Do)**: Deadline today, blocking others, high impact
     - **This Week**: Important but not urgent today
     - **Someday/Maybe**: No deadline, aspirational
  3. Select **3 Priority Tasks** for today:
     - 1 Must Do (highest urgency)
     - 1 Important (moves project forward)
     - 1 Quick Win (takes <15 min, easy to complete)
  4. For each Priority Task, note:
     - When will I do it? (assign to specific time block from calendar review)
     - How long will it take? (estimate)
     - What's the first micro-action? (smallest possible starting step)
  5. Move Priority Tasks to the top of your list (visual prominence)
- **Why**: Without priorities, you'll default to easy/urgent tasks and miss important work
- **Tool**: Google Tasks
- **Output**: 3 clear priorities with time blocks assigned

### 6:42-6:45 AM - Mental Transition Ritual
- **Action**: Close your eyes (or look at wall/floor) and mentally rehearse your first task:
  1. Visualize yourself starting the first Priority Task
  2. Picture the first micro-action (opening file, clicking button, writing first sentence)
  3. Imagine yourself doing it smoothly
  4. Take 3 deep breaths
  5. Say out loud: "I am ready to execute [FIRST TASK]."
- **Why**: Mental rehearsal primes executive function. Saying it out loud reinforces intention.
- **Duration**: 3 minutes (seems long, but critical for neurodivergent brains)
- **Non-Negotiable**: Do not skip this. It's the bridge from planning to action.

**Phase 3 Success Metric**: You know what your day looks like, you have 3 clear priorities, and you've mentally rehearsed starting your first task.

---

## PHASE 4: EXECUTION LAUNCH (6:45-7:00 AM)

**Purpose**: Initiate the first task and create momentum.

### 6:45-6:50 AM - Pre-Launch Check
- **Action**: Run through this checklist before starting work:
  - [ ] Water bottle is full
  - [ ] Headphones are on and comfortable
  - [ ] Phone is on Do Not Disturb
  - [ ] Only necessary tabs/apps are open
  - [ ] I know my first Priority Task
  - [ ] I know the first micro-action for that task
  - [ ] I have time blocked for this task
  - [ ] I feel physically comfortable (not too hot/cold, not hungry)
- **If ANY item is NO**: Fix it before proceeding
- **Why**: Starting with unmet needs guarantees interruption. Fix everything now.

### 6:50-6:55 AM - First Micro-Action Execution
- **Action**:
  1. Set timer for 5 minutes
  2. Execute ONLY the first micro-action of your Priority Task:
     - "Open document" → Open it
     - "Reply to email" → Click reply button
     - "Start coding" → Open code editor and file
     - "Research topic" → Open Google and type search query
  3. When timer ends, assess:
     - Do I want to keep going? (Yes → Continue for 25-minute Pomodoro)
     - Do I need to stop? (Unlikely, but okay → Take 2-min break, then start again)
- **Why**: Starting is the hardest part. Make it so small it feels stupid. Once started, momentum carries you.
- **Tool**: Phone timer

### 6:55-7:00 AM - Momentum Lock
- **Action**: If you're in flow from the micro-action:
  1. Set timer for 25 minutes (one Pomodoro block)
  2. Continue working on Priority Task
  3. Ignore everything else until timer ends
- **If you stopped after micro-action**:
  1. Take 2-minute break (stand, stretch, drink water)
  2. Set timer for 25 minutes
  3. Start again
- **Why**: First Pomodoro sets the tone for the day. Complete it to build momentum.
- **Tool**: Phone timer

**Phase 4 Success Metric**: You have started your first Priority Task and are executing it. You are no longer in transition—you are in execution mode.

## MODE-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

### Solo Execution Mode (Mon 6 PM → Fri 4 PM)
- **Phase 1**: Full protocol (movement, caffeine, no rush)
- **Phase 2**: Maximum environment control (headphones Profile 1.0, all notifications off)
- **Phase 3**: Focus on work tasks (projects, deep work, client deliverables)
- **Phase 4**: Launch directly into 4-hour core work block (7:00 AM - 11:00 AM)

### Family Structure Mode (Fri 4 PM → Mon 6 PM)
- **Phase 1**: Must complete before kids wake (typically 6:30-7:00 AM)
- **Phase 2**: Abbreviated setup (kids will disrupt, accept it)
- **Phase 3**: Focus on family tasks (meal prep, schedule coordination, kid activities)
- **Phase 4**: Launch into family breakfast/morning routine (see Family Mode SOP)
- **Adjustment**: If kids wake early, pause protocol and execute IPI-CSR (Context Switching Recovery)

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### "I snoozed my alarm and woke up at 6:30 AM"
- **Action**: Execute compressed protocol (30 minutes):
  - 6:30-6:32: Water + Pills
  - 6:32-6:37: Skip movement, go straight to caffeine
  - 6:37-6:42: Command Center setup (physical only, skip digital deep-clean)
  - 6:42-6:47: Bubble Shield + Calendar review only (skip Task Triage)
  - 6:47-6:55: Select 1 Priority Task (not 3)
  - 6:55-7:00: Execute first micro-action
- **Note**: Compressed version is survival mode. Return to full protocol tomorrow.

### "I can't wake up at 6:00 AM"
- **Root cause options**:
  1. **Poor sleep**: Check Sleep Anchor compliance (10:30 PM bedtime). If failing, fix that first.
  2. **Sleep debt**: You may need 8+ hours. Move sleep anchor to 10:00 PM for 1 week and reassess.
  3. **Alarm placement**: Put alarm across room so you must stand to turn it off.
  4. **Light therapy**: Use sunrise alarm clock or immediately turn on bright lights upon waking.
- **Fix**: Cannot fix morning without fixing night. Review Evening Sequence in Foundational Protocols.

### "I start checking phone/social media during this protocol"
- **Root cause**: Phone is a dopamine slot machine. Your brain craves easy stimulation.
- **Fix**:
  1. Leave phone in another room during Phase 1-2 (physical separation)
  2. Use separate alarm clock (not phone alarm)
  3. Put phone in grayscale mode (reduces dopamine hit)
  4. Use app blockers (Freedom, Cold Turkey) to block social media 6:00-7:00 AM
- **Why**: Morning screen time destroys executive function. Protect this hour aggressively.

### "I feel anxious during Mental Calibration (Phase 3)"
- **Root cause**: Seeing today's tasks triggers overwhelm.
- **Fix**:
  1. If anxiety spikes, pause and execute 3 minutes of box breathing (from Sensory Overload IPI)
  2. Reduce Priority Tasks from 3 to 1 (just pick the most critical)
  3. Remind yourself: "I only need to start. I don't need to finish everything today."
  4. If chronic: Review Weekly Review Protocol to identify systemic overload patterns
- **Why**: Morning anxiety is often a signal of unsustainable workload. Address root cause, not symptom.

## INTEGRATION WITH EXISTING PROTOCOLS

### Morning Transition → Foundational Protocols
- Phases 1 and 2 execute Water, Pills, and partial Capture protocols
- Morning Transition is an EXTENSION, not a replacement
- All foundational protocols remain mandatory

### Morning Transition → Solo/Family Mode SOPs
- Phase 4 launches directly into mode-specific execution
- Morning Transition is the bridge between sleep and mode activation
- After 7:00 AM, refer to Solo Execution Mode or Family Structure Mode SOPs for hourly breakdown

### Morning Transition → Emergency IPIs
- If you freeze during Phase 3 or 4, execute Executive Dysfunction Emergency Protocol
- If interrupted during protocol, execute Context Switching Recovery Protocol
- If sensory overload occurs, execute Sensory Overload Emergency Protocol

## ADVANCED: MORNING TRANSITION OPTIMIZATION

### Weekly Calibration
- **Action**: During Weekly Review (Phase 5), assess morning protocol performance:
  - Did I complete the full protocol 5+ days this week?
  - What phase consistently fails or gets skipped?
  - What time did I actually start work each day?
- **Optimize**: Adjust timing or sequence based on patterns

### Seasonal Adjustments
- **Winter (darker mornings)**: Add bright light therapy during Phase 1 (sunrise alarm or light box)
- **Summer (earlier sunrise)**: May naturally wake earlier. Maintain 6:00 AM start for consistency.

### High-Performance Mornings
- **When you need maximum output**: Add 10-minute journaling to Phase 3 (brain dump + intention setting)
- **When you have critical deadline**: Skip Task Triage, go straight to single critical task in Phase 4

## SUCCESS METRICS

**You have successfully executed this protocol when:**
- You started your day at 6:00 AM (not 7:00 AM or later)
- You completed all 4 phases without major skips
- You launched into your first Priority Task by 7:00 AM
- You feel physically awake, mentally clear, and ready to execute

**This protocol is NOT designed to:**
- Make you a "morning person" (you may never love mornings—that's okay)
- Replace sleep (if you're exhausted, fix your Sleep Anchor first)
- Guarantee a perfect day (it just gives you the best possible start)

**This is your daily launchpad. Execute it every morning to start from structure, not chaos.**
