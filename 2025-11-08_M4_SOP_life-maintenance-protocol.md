# LIFE MAINTENANCE PROTOCOL (SOP-LM)

**The unglamorous survival tasks that collapse when executive function fails.**

This SOP covers: House cleaning, groceries, personal hygiene. The stuff nobody teaches you when your brain works differently.

---

<h2 id="house-cleaning">SECTION 1: HOUSE CLEANING</h2>

### The Core Problem

Executive dysfunction + visual processing issues = you literally cannot see the mess until it's overwhelming. Then you freeze because "clean the house" is too big and abstract.

### The Solution: Zone-Based Maintenance

**Don't clean the house. Maintain zones on a rotation.**

---

### DAILY MAINTENANCE (15 Minutes Total)

**Rule:** Do these BEFORE they become emergencies.

#### Zone 1: Kitchen (5 minutes)
1. **Dishes Protocol:**
   - Dirty dish touches counter ‚Üí immediately into dishwasher or sink
   - If dishwasher is full ‚Üí run it NOW (don't wait for "later")
   - If sink has dishes ‚Üí set 5-minute timer, wash what you can
2. **Counter Wipe:** One pass with wet rag over counters
3. **Trash Check:** If bag is 80% full ‚Üí take it out (don't wait for overflow)

#### Zone 2: Bathroom (5 minutes)
1. **Sink/Counter:** Wipe down with disinfectant wipe
2. **Toilet:** Spray bowl with cleaner, let sit while you do other zones
3. **Towels:** Hanging? Good. On floor? Pick up, hang or hamper.

#### Zone 3: Living Space (5 minutes)
1. **Flat Surface Rule:** Every flat surface (coffee table, couch, floor) should be clear
2. **Trash Sweep:** Walk through with trash bag, grab obvious garbage
3. **Laundry Pile:** Dirty clothes on floor ‚Üí hamper

**Total: 15 minutes. Set a timer. Stop when it beeps.**

---

### WEEKLY DEEP CLEAN (60 Minutes)

**Schedule:** Sunday 9-10 AM (or pick a consistent time)

#### Week 1 Focus: Kitchen + Bathroom
**Kitchen (30 min):**
1. Empty dishwasher/load dirty dishes (10 min)
2. Wipe down all counters and stovetop (10 min)
3. Sweep/mop floor (10 min)

**Bathroom (30 min):**
1. Scrub toilet (inside and outside) (10 min)
2. Scrub sink and counter (10 min)
3. Shower/tub: spray with cleaner, scrub, rinse (10 min)

#### Week 2 Focus: Living Space + Bedroom
**Living Space (30 min):**
1. Vacuum or sweep floors (15 min)
2. Dust surfaces (TV stand, shelves, window sills) (10 min)
3. Organize clutter (put things back where they belong) (5 min)

**Bedroom (30 min):**
1. Change bed sheets (10 min)
2. Vacuum floor (10 min)
3. Organize nightstand/dresser (10 min)

#### Week 3 Focus: Laundry + Floors
**Laundry (30 min active, rest is machine time):**
1. Collect all dirty clothes from hampers/floor (5 min)
2. Sort lights/darks (5 min)
3. Load 1st wash (5 min)
4. While wash runs: vacuum/sweep all floors (15 min)
5. Transfer to dryer, load 2nd wash
6. Fold and put away when dry (15 min)

**Floors (30 min):**
1. Vacuum all carpeted areas (20 min)
2. Mop hard floors (10 min)

#### Week 4 Focus: Kitchen Deep Clean + Misc
**Kitchen Deep Clean (30 min):**
1. Wipe inside microwave (5 min)
2. Wipe down fridge exterior and handles (5 min)
3. Clean stovetop/oven exterior (10 min)
4. Take out all trash/recycling (5 min)
5. Wipe down cabinet fronts (5 min)

**Miscellaneous (30 min):**
1. Dust all surfaces you missed (10 min)
2. Clean mirrors (5 min)
3. Wipe door handles and light switches (5 min)
4. Declutter one junk drawer/cabinet (10 min)

---

### EMERGENCY RESET: House Is Disaster

**When everything has collapsed and you can't see the floor.**

**Time Required:** 2-3 hours  
**When to Deploy:** House is actively stressing you out

#### Phase 1: Trash Blitz (15 minutes)
1. Get large trash bag
2. Walk through every room
3. Throw away ONLY obvious trash (food wrappers, empty bottles, paper garbage)
4. Take bag to outdoor bin immediately

#### Phase 2: Dishes Purge (20 minutes)
1. Collect every dish from every room
2. Bring to kitchen
3. Load dishwasher completely
4. Start dishwasher
5. Hand wash any remaining dishes (or soak in sink)

#### Phase 3: Laundry Bomb (15 minutes active)
1. Collect all dirty clothes
2. Start first load immediately
3. Set timer to move to dryer in 45 minutes

#### Phase 4: Floor Clear (30 minutes)
1. Pick up everything on floor in each room
2. Sort into categories: Trash (throw away), Clothes (hamper), Stuff (box for later sorting)
3. Don't organize yet‚Äîjust clear walking paths

#### Phase 5: Surface Reset (30 minutes)
1. Wipe down kitchen counters
2. Wipe down bathroom sink/toilet
3. Clear coffee table/couch
4. Make bed (or at least straighten blankets)

#### Phase 6: Maintenance Lock-In
1. Set recurring phone alarm: "Daily 15-min clean" at 8 PM
2. For next 7 days, do 15-minute daily maintenance (see above)
3. Schedule next Weekly Deep Clean on calendar

<h2 id="groceries">SECTION 2: GROCERIES</h2>

### The Core Problem

Grocery shopping requires: planning meals, making lists, driving to store, navigating crowded aisles, decision-making, checkout interaction, unloading/putting away. That's 7+ executive function tasks. No wonder we eat cereal for dinner.

### The Solution: Template-Based Shopping

**Stop planning meals. Use a template list.**

---

**Print this. Laminate it. Check boxes when low. Shop when 50% of boxes are checked.**

- [ ] Eggs (18-count)
- [ ] Rotisserie chicken (pre-cooked)
- [ ] Ground beef or turkey (1 lb)
- [ ] Canned tuna or salmon (4 cans)
- [ ] Deli meat (turkey/ham, 1 lb)
- [ ] Frozen chicken breasts (2 lb bag)
- [ ] Peanut butter or almond butter (1 jar)

#### Carbs (Pick 3-4)

- [ ] Rice (1 bag or instant cups)
- [ ] Tortillas (1 pack)
- [ ] Oatmeal (1 container)
- [ ] Crackers (1-2 boxes)
- [ ] Cereal (1-2 boxes)

#### Vegetables (Pick 4-5)

---

### SHOPPING EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Goal:** Get in, get out, minimize overwhelm.

#### Pre-Shop Setup (5 minutes)
1. Take photo of template with checked boxes
2. Set phone to Do Not Disturb
3. Headphones on (Bubble Shield Profile 2.0)
4. Choose low-traffic time: Tuesday/Wednesday 10 AM or 8 PM

#### Store Navigation (45-60 minutes)
1. **Perimeter First:** Start at produce, loop to dairy, meat, bakery
2. **Aisles Second:** Hit only aisles you need (pasta, canned goods, cereal)
3. **Frozen Last:** Grab frozen items right before checkout
4. **Checkout Strategy:** Self-checkout if available (less social interaction)

#### Post-Shop Recovery (20 minutes)
1. Unload groceries onto counter
2. **Put away perishables first:** Meat, dairy, frozen (everything else can wait)
3. Set 10-minute timer: put away as much as possible
4. When timer beeps: Stop. Leave rest on counter if needed. You did the important part.

---

### MEAL PREP: NO-COOK AND LOW-EFFORT OPTIONS

**You don't need to cook elaborate meals. You need to eat.**

#### No-Cook Meals (5 minutes or less)
- **Sandwich:** Deli meat + cheese + bread + mayo
- **Cheese & Crackers Plate:** Cheese slices + crackers + apple slices
- **Cereal:** Bowl + cereal + milk
- **Yogurt Parfait:** Yogurt cup + granola + berries
- **PB&J:** Peanut butter + jelly + bread
- **Snack Dinner:** String cheese + crackers + carrots + fruit

#### Low-Effort Hot Meals (10-15 minutes)
- **Pasta:** Boil pasta (8 min) + dump jar of sauce + microwave frozen meatballs
- **Quesadilla:** Tortilla + cheese + microwave 1 minute or pan-fry 3 minutes
- **Scrambled Eggs:** Eggs + butter + pan (5 min) + toast
- **Ramen Upgrade:** Ramen + frozen vegetables + egg stirred in
- **Rotisserie Chicken Meal:** Pre-cooked chicken (pull meat off) + microwave rice + frozen veggies
- **Mac and Cheese:** Box mac + milk + butter (8 min) + add frozen peas

#### Backup Emergency Meals (Zero Effort)
- Frozen pizza (oven 15 min)
- Microwaveable dinner (4 min)
- Canned soup + crackers (3 min)
- Frozen burritos (microwave 2 min)

**Rule:** Eating something > eating nothing. Frozen pizza is better than skipping meals.

<h2 id="hygiene">SECTION 3: PERSONAL HYGIENE</h2>

### The Core Problem

Sensory issues + executive dysfunction + depression = hygiene tasks feel impossible. The water temperature, the texture of soap, the effort of standing, the drying off, the getting dressed again... it's not laziness. It's system overload.

### The Solution: Tiered Hygiene System

**Not every day requires a full shower. Match hygiene to your capacity.**

---

### TIER 1: MINIMAL FUNCTION DAY (5 Minutes)

**When you can't shower but need to function.**

1. **Face Wipe:** Use face wipe or wet washcloth on face
2. **Deodorant:** Apply deodorant
3. **Teeth:** Brush teeth (2 min) OR use mouthwash if you can't brush
4. **Hair:** Dry shampoo if greasy, or pull back in ponytail/hat
5. **Clothes:** Clean underwear minimum (even if you re-wear shirt/pants)

**This is enough. You did the bare minimum to not feel gross. That counts.**

---

### TIER 2: SINK WASH (10 Minutes)

**When shower feels impossible but you want to feel cleaner.**

1. **Face:** Wash face with soap and water
2. **Armpits:** Wet washcloth + soap, scrub armpits
3. **Groin:** Wet washcloth + soap, quick wash
4. **Feet:** If they feel gross, quick rinse in sink or wipe down
5. **Deodorant:** Apply after washing
6. **Teeth:** Brush teeth (2 min)
7. **Hair:** If needed, lean over sink and rinse hair, or use dry shampoo
8. **Fresh Clothes:** Put on clean underwear + shirt at minimum

**You cleaned the important parts. This is valid hygiene.**

---

### TIER 3: QUICK SHOWER (15 Minutes)

**When you can manage a shower but need it fast.**

#### Pre-Shower Setup (2 minutes)
1. Set phone timer for 10 minutes
2. Put fresh clothes + towel within reach
3. Start water, get it to tolerable temperature
4. Undress

#### In-Shower Protocol (10 minutes)
1. **Wet hair and body** (1 min)
2. **Shampoo hair** (1 min scrub, 1 min rinse)
3. **Body wash:** Focus on armpits, groin, feet (3 min)
4. **Face wash:** Quick scrub with hands or washcloth (1 min)
5. **Rinse everything** (2 min)
6. **Done.** Exit when timer beeps.

**Skip:** Conditioner (unless hair is tangled), shaving, extended rinsing, standing under water zoning out

#### Post-Shower (3 minutes)
1. Towel dry (don't worry about perfect dryness)
2. Deodorant
3. Fresh clothes
4. Air-dry hair or quick towel scrunch (no blow dryer required)

---

### TIER 4: FULL HYGIENE ROUTINE (30-45 Minutes)

**When you have capacity for complete maintenance.**

#### Pre-Shower (5 minutes)
1. Gather: Towel, clean clothes, hair brush, deodorant, lotion
2. Start water, adjust temperature
3. Play music or podcast if helpful
4. Undress

#### Shower (20 minutes)
1. Wet hair and body (2 min)
2. Shampoo + rinse (3 min)
3. Conditioner, leave in while washing body (wash body 5 min, rinse conditioner 2 min)
4. Body wash full scrub: armpits, groin, feet, legs, arms, torso (5 min)
5. Face wash (2 min)
6. Optional: Shave if needed (5 min)
7. Final rinse (1 min)

#### Post-Shower (10 minutes)
1. Towel dry thoroughly
2. Deodorant
3. Lotion if skin is dry
4. Brush/comb hair
5. Fresh clothes (including socks and underwear)
6. Trim nails if needed

#### Hair Care (5 minutes)
- Air dry OR blow dry if needed
- Brush when dry or mostly dry
- Style if desired (optional)

#### Teeth (5 minutes)
- Brush (2 min)
- Floss (2 min)
- Mouthwash (1 min)

---

### HYGIENE FREQUENCY GUIDE

**Adjust based on your actual capacity, not "shoulds."**

#### Daily Minimum (Tier 1 or 2)
- Brush teeth once (morning or night)
- Apply deodorant
- Change underwear
- Face wipe or wash

#### Every 2-3 Days (Tier 2 or 3)
- Full body wash (shower or sink)
- Wash hair OR dry shampoo
- Fresh clothes (shirt + pants)

#### Weekly (Tier 4)
- Full shower with hair wash
- Trim nails
- Change bed sheets
- Laundry (see house cleaning section)

**If you miss days:** Don't spiral. Just do Tier 1 today, Tier 2 tomorrow, Tier 3 when you can. Progress > perfection.

---

### SENSORY ACCOMMODATIONS

**If showers are hard due to sensory issues:**

- **Water Pressure:** Adjust to lower pressure, or use detachable shower head
- **Temperature:** Lukewarm is fine (doesn't have to be hot)
- **Time of Day:** Shower when you have most energy (morning, afternoon, night‚Äîdoesn't matter)
- **Lighting:** Dim lights or shower in dark if bright lights are overwhelming
- **Sound:** Music or white noise to drown out water sound if it's too much
- **Texture:** Use soft washcloth instead of loofah, or just hands + body wash
- **Soap:** Unscented or low-scent soap if smells trigger you
- **Standing:** Sit on shower stool or edge of tub if standing is exhausting

<h2 id="integration">SECTION 4: INTEGRATION WITH DAILY PROTOCOLS</h2>

### Morning Routine
- **6:00-6:30 AM:** Tier 1 hygiene minimum (face, teeth, deodorant)
- **6:30-7:00 AM:** Kitchen 5-min zone maintenance

### Evening Routine
- **9:00-9:15 PM:** Daily 15-min house maintenance (kitchen, bathroom, living space)
- **10:00-10:15 PM:** Tier 1 or 2 hygiene before bed (teeth, face wash)

### Weekly Schedule
- **Sunday 9-10 AM:** Weekly Deep Clean
- **Wednesday 6-7 PM:** Grocery shopping (or pick another consistent day)
- **Every 2-3 days:** Tier 3 Quick Shower (or as needed)

---

<h2 id="emergency">EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS</h2>

### When You've Skipped Everything for Days/Weeks

**Don't try to catch up all at once. Triage.**

1. **Today:** Tier 1 hygiene (5 min) + Trash Blitz (15 min)
2. **Tomorrow:** Tier 2 hygiene (10 min) + Dishes Purge (20 min)
3. **Day 3:** Tier 3 shower (15 min) + Grocery delivery order (online, 15 min)
4. **Day 4:** Resume daily 15-min house maintenance
5. **Day 5:** Schedule Weekly Deep Clean for upcoming weekend

**Don't judge yourself. Systems fail. This protocol exists to reboot them.**
